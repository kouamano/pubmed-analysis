Figure \ref{fig:gr9} shows the number of journals publishing highly cited papers. Both the total number of such journals and the number of newly appearing journals publishing highly cited papers increase sharply after the cumulative cohort for 1946–2010, consistent with the timing of the previously noted rapid increase in the number of highly cited papers.

To examine the impact of journals publishing highly cited papers, Figure \ref{fig:gr10} presents their proportion among all journals. No clear temporal trend is observed, and only gradual fluctuations can be seen.

To assess turnover among journals publishing highly cited papers, Figure \ref{fig:gr11} shows the proportion of newly appearing such journals among all journals publishing highly cited papers. No consistent temporal trend is observed, and substantial fluctuations are evident.
