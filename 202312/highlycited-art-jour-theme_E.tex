In this study, we define high citation papers as the group of papers whose citation counts constitute the top1% of all citations within each cumulative cohort. Journals that publish these papers are called high citation paper journals, and the MeSH descriptors assigned to high citation papers are termed high citation themes.

We also consider the set of papers that cite the high citation papers; the MeSH descriptors attached to these citing papers are defined as high citation paper citation themes.

More generally, MeSH descriptors assigned to any paper are referred to as research themes. When a citation relationship exists between two papers, the MeSH descriptors attached to the cited paper are called cited themes, and those attached to the citing paper are called citing themes.
