From the above, we can infer that highly cited papers have the following characteristics: (1) Highly cited papers tend to be cited from different topics. This can be anticipated from the most frequent topics shown in Table6, and the degree of pivot displayed in Figure\ref{fig:meshpivot} also supports this. (2) Highly cited papers have concentrated research topics, and the papers that cite them likewise have concentrated research topics. This is suggested by Figure\ref{fig:arr2}. (3) The thematic diversity of highly cited papers is low synchronically but not necessarily low diachronically. This is inferred from the fact that, although Figure17 shows lower entropy compared with other groups, Figure\ref{fig:gr15} exhibits a level of pivot comparable to that of other groups.
